About

I am a Johns Hopkins University Molecular and Cellular Biology graduate with a diverse professional background spanning data analysis, laboratory work, medicine, and teaching. I have a keen interest in leveraging technology for broad societal benefits, with a current emphasis on advancing my proficiency in software design. My aptitude for rapidly acquiring new skills within related domains, coupled with my teaching expertise, allows me to rapidly gain and transfer knowledge to colleagues.

My recent endeavors include completing a 6-month software engineering bootcamp with App Academy after my first year of medical school, affording me the opportunity to delve deeper into my passion for technology. Before entering medical school, I utilized Python, Excel, and MatLab to develop optimized, ready-to-use food recipes from an FDA database of nutritional information for various ingredients. This project aimed to address nutritional challenges in impoverished areas of Southeast Asia. Witnessing the impact of technology in this context motivated me to pivot my focus towards software and data analysis. I aspire to steer my career in this direction, eager to contribute to the development of software that has a positive impact on others.
