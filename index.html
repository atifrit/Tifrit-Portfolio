ABOUT

I graduated from Johns Hopkins University with a degree in Molecular and Cellular Biology and professional experience in data analysis, laboratory work, medicine, and teaching. I am particularly interested in using my skills in software design for large scale social benefit in healthcare. My aptitude for learning new skills quickly, coupled with my teaching expertise, allows me to communicate knowledge well with colleagues.
After my first year of medical school, I completed a six-month software engineering bootcamp with App Academy. This was something I took on because I had always been interested in both medicine and technology, and what I had learned in medical school reaffirmed to me the possibilities of combining the two. Prior to beginning medical school, I had spent two years working on a team of engineers using Python, Excel, and MatLab to develop tailored recipe recommendations for impoverished peoples in Southeast Asia. Our software cross-referenced nutritional information from Food & Drug Administration databases with statistics on regionally available ingredients to ensure that recipes were maximally helpful. Witnessing the impact of technology in this context motivated me to consider alternative ways to impact world health, outside of just being a doctor. I remain hopeful that through the development of socially conscious software, I can continue to make a positive impact on others.

PROJECTS

CanaryHood is a real time stock simulator inspired by RobinHood. It allows users to manage a portfolio of stocks as well as a watchlist of interest for over 100 different companies. This project was made in collaboration with two other engineers in order to improve our abilities to utilize, display, and alter large data sets. Using third party packages, we displayed generated stock data over user-selected time periods as well as a portfolio graph indicating the performance of stocks owned by the user.
GorillaBuff Auto is a prototype of a future production website for a local vehicle detailing business. It allows users to store virtual currency and make appointments for their cars. This site has secure user authentication utilizing technologies such as bcrypt to secure passwords and offer security to users signing up. This site is currently in development, and the production site is projected to feature a payment gateway, google maps API, and updated design per the request of the business owner.
